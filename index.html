Our goal

Starting at October 2022, from Grade 12 STEM 3PM of the National Teachers College, we formed as a group of 7 students that aims to inform our fellow students the right way to engage in the cyberspace. We design an informative content which helps our viewers to understand how to avoid online misinformations and as well as promote online etiquette, Netiquette. Social media vastly impacts our ability to spread information, both f ake and fact. Fake news is false or misleading information presented as news. We are starting this blog to increase the avoidance of spreading fake news. We want to help all the people who are victims of fake news. When I was on my way to school, someone told me that our class got suspended alongside the source where he got the information. We are unable to attend class due to believing in fake news. This incident helped us learn from our mistakes; For us to avoid encountering the error we have dealt with, we are here to promote proper netiquette.

netiquette

Has information dissemination progressed or regressed? In the 21st century, spreading news has evolved greatly from papers, TV, and now, on the internet. It is now convenient for all to be updated anytime and anywhere as social media and the internet has been made to be easily accessed by everyone; but was it really a big help, or meant to deceive the public? In the Philippines, there was a survey conducted by SWS in December 2021 that shows 51% of Filipinos find it hard to spot fake news. Well, the citizens are not the one to blame, but those people who roll out such disinformation. For those people who peddles fake news, they are the ones who don't know how to utilize the virtual world in a good manner. In some places/things we do, there are proper ways of what to do there, or how to do something -- and that is called etiquette, well we don't only have it in physical or in-person activities, but also on the internet. It is called Netiquette, and one of the netiquettes is to 'Think before you Click'. Before sharing such information, think and ask yourself first if it is true, credible, or reliable; if it will help others or would deceive them into believing in false information. In this present-day, our world doesn't only revolve around the real-time moments, but also virtually. Let us be the good person we are in person, and on the internet. Be responsible for everything you share, and practice proper netiquette.
